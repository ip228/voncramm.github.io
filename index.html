Welcome to Redbud Cooperative!


Established 1953

With 34 members, Redbud Cooperative is the largest house owned by Cornell University.

We are a diverse group of graduate and undergraduate students with a wide range of backgrounds and interests. Our goal is to be a community that supports and enriches its members through thoughtful interaction, responsibility and generosity. At most times of the day, there are people around to engage in conversation or perhaps a game of pool. Often in winter, people are drawn to the fireplace in the living room, and when warm weather arrives they hang out in the back yard.

At the cooperative:

- Rent includes room, all utilities, full meal plan and laundry.
- Crammie-made house dinners happen six nights a week, but food for the remaining week is always available.
- Each member shares responsibility for cooking, cleaning, and house maintenance.
- Bi-weekly house meetings are held to determine house policy.

Membership

Membership to reside in Redbud is open to all full-time Cornell students, including undergraduate, graduate, international and transfer students. Redbud welcomes and strives to provide safe spaces for people of color, queer individuals, students with physical or mental disabilities, students with financial burdens, and students with other marginalized identities.

  • Rooms

  • Rooming Rates

    The rates per semester are:
    1. Single: $2400 (~$600/month)
    2. Double: $2100 (~$525/month)
    3. Triple: $1800 (~$450/month)
